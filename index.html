Mission

FOR A FREE PALESTINE! END THE GENOCIDE!

Liberation Station was born out of the urgency to expose how Israel and its supporting structures use culture and music to whitewash their international image and normalize an ongoing genocide. This strategy of cultural whitewashing isn’t new, we’ve seen it in several recent events, but we’ve also seen how collective action and boycott can dismantle it.

The immediate trigger for our initiative was HÖR’s Weekender in Lisbon, a platform that claims neutrality but, in practice, censored artists and silenced voices that were supportive of Palestine. We decided to respond creatively and collectively by launching our own initiative on the exact same dates, using the same mediums: music, clubbing, streaming, and community radio, all while running a fundraising campaign for Gaza.

From September 30 to October 5, we’ll be organizing 9 events across 5 cities, from north to south of Portugal. These include live streams and gatherings in key underground and alternative venues. Everything is done voluntarily, non-profit, with one single purpose: to unite, raise our voices, and channel resources to those who need them most.

Our project is a space of active solidarity. All funds raised go directly to families in Gaza. Through our partners, we’re in contact with several families and have identified 4 in urgent need due to critical situations. They have our guaranteed support first. Afterwards, each additional €100 raised will allow us to support another family. Every transfer will be documented and published to ensure full transparency.

Liberation Station is music, solidarity, and resistance. It proves that culture doesn’t have to be complicit. It can be a tool for truth-telling, community, and struggle.

FAQ

Who is behind Liberation Station?

We’re a collective of over thirty people across Portugal, most of us connected to music as artists, producers, and more. We are fully volunteer-run, non-profit, and came together to organize an event that both exposes the use of culture as Zionist propaganda and directly supports Palestinian families in Gaza.

Who receives the donations?

All donations made through our events and website go directly to Associação Juvenil CulturMais, NIPC 510862110, a legally registered association in Portugal. This campaign has been formally approved by the association’s board, which authorized the collection and distribution of funds.

Why are donations going through an association?

For security and transparency. By using a legally registered Portuguese association, we avoid handling money through our personal bank accounts. The association’s board officially approved this campaign, and every transfer to families will be documented and published.

Where do the funds go?

The association distributes 100% of the money raised to families in Gaza through their personal GoFundMe campaigns and similar platforms, which are currently the main channels available for them to receive aid.

How are donations allocated?

Through our partners who maintain direct contact with 28 families, we identified 4 in the most urgent situations (due to health complications, loss of relatives, or displacement with heavy costs). These are our initial priority. Then, for every additional €100 raised, we’ll support another family.

What if more is raised than expected?

The more we raise, the more families we can support. If we exceed the amount needed for the 28 families, we’ll increase the support provided to each one.

How does sending money actually help families in Gaza?

The cost of living in Gaza has skyrocketed due to the blockade and lack of aid. A kilo of rice, for example, can cost up to $40. Beyond food and essentials, families face enormous expenses for displacement, medical care, replacing destroyed belongings, and other urgent needs. Every contribution helps relieve these hardships, giving families the means to survive and preserve their dignity under impossible conditions.

How do I know the money really reaches the families?

All transfers will be documented and later published on our platforms to ensure complete transparency.

Can I donate without attending the events?

Yes! Just use the payment methods available on our website. Every contribution, no matter how small, has a direct impact.

Can I donate directly to families instead of through you?

Yes. We want to be fully transparent. The families we support have their own GoFundMe and similar campaigns. If you prefer, you can donate directly here. There are also collectives and organizations doing vital work on the ground, and every contribution makes a difference: Seeds of Hope, Gaza Soup Kitchen, Sameer Project, and others.

How else can I help besides donating?

Spread the word about Liberation Station, share our fundraising campaign on social media, and come to our events. Solidarity is built collectively :)
